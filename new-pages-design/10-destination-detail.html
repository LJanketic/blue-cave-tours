Hvar island

Croatia's sunniest island — lavender fields running down to the sea, a hilltop fortress you can walk to in twenty minutes, and water so clear you can see the bottom from the boat. It's busy in July and August, but arrive by tender from the water and you sidestep the crowds entirely.

Highlights

Some of the clearest water on the Adriatic
Venetian fortress with panoramic views
Lavender fields in June and early July
Local plavac mali wine worth tasting
Hidden coves only reachable by boat
Lively old town squares in the evening

Best time to visit

Peak season
Good
Off season

May, June and September are the sweet spot — warm enough to swim, calm enough to sail, and far fewer crowds than peak summer.

Insider tips

1The fortress walk takes about 20 minutes from the main square and the view from the top is worth every step. Go in the late afternoon for the best light.
2The Pakleni islands just offshore are where the good swimming is — the main town beach is pretty but crowded. We always anchor off Palmižana.
3Arriving by boat means you dock right in the heart of the old town. Guests coming by ferry land a short walk away — another reason the water approach wins.
