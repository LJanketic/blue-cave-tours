We're small by design

Adriatic Sails started in 2015 with one boat, one captain, and a strong belief that the best way to see the Dalmatian coast is from the water — slowly, in a small group, with someone who actually knows where to go.

Ten years later, we still cap our group tours at 12 guests. We still turn down the big agencies. And we still take every charter ourselves, with our own captains, on our own boats. That's not nostalgia — it's just the only way this works properly.

How we got here

2015

First charter, first season

Marko Blažević launches Adriatic Sails with a single restored wooden vessel and a waitlist of family and friends. Sunset tours only, 8 guests maximum.

2017

Second boat, island routes

Growing demand for day trips leads to the purchase of a second vessel and the launch of the Elafiti island route — still one of our most popular tours.

2019

Private charter programme

After years of requests, we formalise private charters with fully customisable routes, optional catering, and photography packages.

2021

Through the quiet years

We ran reduced operations during the pandemic, focused on local guests and day-return bookings, and came out the other side with a waitlist for 2022.

2025

Ten years on the water

Still independent. Still capping tours at 12. Still run by the same team. This year we welcomed our 20,000th guest.

What we believe in

Small groups, always

We cap every tour at 12. Not because we have to — because it's the only way it works properly.

Low-impact on the sea

Reef-safe sunscreen required. We don't anchor on coral. We take rubbish back to port.

Local all the way

Local captains, local crew, local suppliers. The money stays in Dubrovnik — that matters to us.

No compromise on safety

If conditions aren't right, we don't go. Full stop. The sea will still be there tomorrow.
